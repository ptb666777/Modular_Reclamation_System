6. The Residual Stream (Unrecoverable)
Description & Source: The final, heavily mixed, and contaminated fines (small particulate matter), degraded fibrous materials, contaminated soil/sediment (from the liner), and any material deemed economically non-viable for immediate processing.

Key Challenge: Must be isolated and contained as it still holds a medium-to-high environmental risk due to mixed chemical contamination (leaching potential) but lacks the specific high-value of CTH. This material represents the final volume reduction for the site.

Vessel Required Specification: The High-Integrity Burial Cask (Permanent Containment):

Integrity: Ultra-durable, thick-walled steel/polymer composite designed for permanent, long-term geological containment. Must be corrosion-resistant for 100+ years.

Environment: Designed to accept a solidifying agent (e.g., cementitious material or polymer) in situ before final sealing to prevent future settling or leaching.

Logistics: Standardized size for dense, vertical stacking in the final repository. Each cask receives a unique, immutable digital tag linked to the DSM coordinates of its final resting place.

Goal: To bank material that is either too chemically complex, too low-concentration, or too contaminated for current economic processing, but contains critical or high-value elements we refuse to bury permanently. This is the long-term asset inventory.
