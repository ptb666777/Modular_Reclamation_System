You are right that all processes create some waste. The goal is to minimize it and manage it responsibly.

Carbon Waste: When you process plastics or mixed organics via pyrolysis (heating in the absence of oxygen) or gasification, you can produce fuel, but you also produce a carbon-rich solid called char or biochar.

The Strategy: This isn't necessarily waste. Biochar is valuable. It can be used as a soil amendment to improve water retention and sequester carbon long-term.

Filter Mediums (The Sludge from Cleaning): Your filtration units (for LFG and leachate) will accumulate toxic compounds.

The Strategy: This needs to be the absolute final, highly controlled waste stream. The heavy metals and persistent toxins are concentrated into a small, stable sludge. This is typically sent to a designated secure hazardous waste landfill for permanent, safe disposal. The volume is vastly smaller than the original landfill volume, making it a manageable problem.
