III. Decentralized Communications & Logic
Since there is no "Nexus," coordination between the two Mover Units, the Worm, and the Processing Hubs relies on a simple, robust protocol:

Broadcast and Listen: All nodes (Mechs, Movers, Hubs) continuously broadcast their Status (Available/Busy/Error) and Demand (I need vessel X, I am ready to receive vessel Y).

Autonomous Scheduling: The Mover Unit operates on a simple, autonomous scheduling algorithm: It prioritizes the Highest Priority Material (SUHP/HVIC) demand, then the Highest Volume Need (BRS/NRO), and finally, the Tonnage-Metric Need (Leachate/BCM).

Collision Resolution: If both Movers identify the same highest priority task, the conflict is resolved based on proximity, minimizing travel distance to ensure low operational cost.

This approach ensures the two Mover Units work at maximum efficiency, driven by real-time localized data and a non-profit-maximizing prioritization schedule.
